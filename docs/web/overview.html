Overview

A New Model for Web3 Connections

Sereus Fabric rethinks how users connect and share data on the web. Instead of broadcasting your identity to a global network, Sereus creates small, private threads—invitation-only subnets where connections form through mutual consent and existing trust.

What Are Threads?

A thread is the fundamental building block of Sereus. It represents:

  • A trusted subnet: Threads connect two or more users who have explicitly consented to share a common space.
  • A shared SQL database: Each thread contains a database accessible by all participants, with access controlled by role-based permissions defined by the thread creator or group.
  • A privacy boundary: Identities are shared only among thread participants. There is no global identity registry or directory.
  • A collaboration space: Thread databases can contain any SQL schema, supporting diverse applications from messaging to complex business logic.

Key Principles

Consent-Based Identity

Unlike traditional web platforms where your identity is globally searchable, Sereus identities exist only within the context of specific threads. When you join a thread, you share your identity only with that thread's participants. No central authority tracks your presence or connections.

Out-of-Band Trust

Threads form based on existing knowledge or trust between participants. Credentials and initial connections are established out of band—perhaps through a QR code, an email, or an in-person exchange. This eliminates the need for certificate authorities or SSL-style trust hierarchies.

Shared Data, Distributed Storage

Each thread's database is distributed across the devices (cadres) of its participants. Data is sharded and distributed for resilience. All participants see a consistent view of the data, with access controlled by their assigned roles within the thread.

Invitation-Only Joining

New members join threads only by invitation from existing participants. This creates a natural barrier against spam, unsolicited messages, and unwanted connections. You receive communications only from threads you've explicitly joined.

How It Differs from Traditional Web

Traditional web applications typically rely on:

  • Central servers that broker all connections
  • Global identity systems (usernames, email addresses, phone numbers)
  • Platform owners who control access and data
  • Certificate authorities to establish trust

Sereus replaces this model with:

  • Direct peer-to-peer connections via libp2p
  • Thread-scoped identities known only to participants
  • User-owned data distributed across trusted devices
  • Out-of-band trust establishment without central authorities

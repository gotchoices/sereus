Overview

A New Model for Web3 Connections

Sereus Fabric rethinks how users connect and share data on the web. Instead of broadcasting your identity to a global network, Sereus creates small, private strands—invitation-only subnets where connections form through mutual consent and existing trust.

What Are Strands?

A strand is the fundamental building block of Sereus. It represents:

  • A trusted subnet: Strands connect two or more users who have explicitly consented to share a common space.
  • A shared SQL database: Each strand contains a database accessible by all participants, with access controlled by role-based permissions defined by the strand creator or group.
  • A privacy boundary: Identities are shared only among strand participants. There is no global identity registry or directory.
  • A collaboration space: Strand databases can contain any SQL schema, supporting diverse applications from messaging to complex business logic.

Key Principles

Consent-Based Identity

Unlike traditional web platforms where your identity is globally searchable, Sereus identities exist only within the context of specific strands. When you join a strand, you share your identity only with that strand's participants. No central authority tracks your presence or connections.

Out-of-Band Trust

Strands form based on existing knowledge or trust between participants. Credentials and initial connections are established out of band—perhaps through a QR code, an email, or an in-person exchange. This eliminates the need for certificate authorities or SSL-style trust hierarchies.

Shared Data, Distributed Storage

Each strand's database is distributed across a cohort—all the devices belonging to the strand's participants. Every user contributes their personal device cluster (their cadre) to the strand's cohort. Data is sharded and distributed across the cohort for resilience, and all participants see a consistent view of the data, with access controlled by their assigned roles within the strand.

Invitation-Only Joining

New members join strands only by invitation from existing participants. This creates a natural barrier against spam, unsolicited messages, and unwanted connections. You receive communications only from strands you've explicitly joined.

How It Differs from Traditional Web

Traditional web applications typically rely on:

  • Central servers that broker all connections
  • Global identity systems (usernames, email addresses, phone numbers)
  • Platform owners who control access and data
  • Certificate authorities to establish trust

Sereus replaces this model with:

  • Direct peer-to-peer connections via libp2p
  • Strand-scoped identities known only to participants
  • User-owned data distributed across trusted devices
  • Out-of-band trust establishment without central authorities

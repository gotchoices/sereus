Use Case: Classifieds, Auctions & E-Commerce

The Centralization Problem

In addition to building a private social media timeline, a user (or company) might want to build a for-sale spaceâ€”a place to list goods or services for potential buyers. However, simply listing items on an unknown page somewhere on the Internet isn't useful. The critical challenge is: how do buyers find your listings?

The Current Marketplace Landscape

Current web topology heavily favors centralization around a few large players:

  • Shoppers for new goods go to Amazon because they know so many sellers list there
  • Shoppers for used goods go to eBay for the same reason
  • Other centralized services (like Facebook Marketplace) are making significant inroads

This creates several problems:

  • Platform fees: Sellers pay significant percentages to the platform
  • Platform control: Your listings can be removed or demoted at the platform's discretion
  • Biased search: A Google search doesn't always yield the items you need, but rather the items being most aggressively advertised to you
  • Lock-in: Your reputation and transaction history are trapped within each platform
  • No neutrality: Platforms favor their own interests and advertisers over optimal buyer-seller matches

The Sereus Alternative: Neutral Ground

Sereus Fabric offers a neutral foundation where multiple commercial services can operate, each selling specialized services such as:

  • Publicity: Services that ensure buyers will find your goods and services
  • Indexes: Search services that help shoppers find what they need
  • Shipping: Services that guarantee delivery of purchased goods
  • Escrow: Services that ensure payment happens only when goods/services are rendered

Crucially, sellers and buyers choose their own service providers for each function, based on reputation and preference. They're not locked into a single platform's bundled offerings.

How It Works

Decoupled Services

In the Sereus model:

  • A seller can use the publicity service of their choice
  • A buyer can use the search service of their choice
  • Both will still find each other, because these services work on a neutral foundation
  • Services like shipping and escrow can be chosen by mutual consent based on reputation

Portable Trust and Reputation

Rather than reputation being locked into a single platform:

  • Sellers build reputation across transactions in different venues
  • Buyers can reference a seller's transaction history across services
  • Reputation services can compete on fairness and accuracy
  • Trust is portable and not controlled by a platform

Strand-Based Transactions

Each transaction might involve several strands:

  1. Listing strand: Seller publishes item details; publicity service makes it discoverable
  2. Transaction strand: Buyer and seller create a shared strand to negotiate terms
  3. Escrow strand: Buyer, seller, and escrow service share a strand to manage payment
  4. Shipping strand: Buyer, seller, and shipper coordinate delivery
  5. Reputation strand: Transaction outcome is recorded for future reference

Advantages Over Platform Marketplaces

  • No platform rent: Service providers compete on value, not platform monopoly
  • Buyer-seller direct connection: Once connected through search, parties communicate peer-to-peer
  • Service flexibility: Choose publicity, search, shipping, and escrow independently
  • Portable reputation: Your transaction history isn't trapped in a single platform
  • Neutral search: Search services compete on relevance, not advertiser revenue
  • No arbitrary delistings: Platforms can't unilaterally remove your listings or ban your account
  • Privacy options: Transactions can be as public or private as participants choose
  • Fair competition: New service providers can enter the market without platform permission

Example Services on Sereus

Discovery/Index Services

Compete on search relevance, category coverage, and user experience. Earn revenue through subscriptions or commissions, not by extracting monopoly rents.

Publicity Services

Help sellers reach buyers across multiple search indexes. Success measured by actual buyer connections, not just ad impressions.

Escrow Services

Compete on fee structure, dispute resolution fairness, and speed. Reputation matters because buyers and sellers choose explicitly.

Shipping Services

Traditional and new shipping providers can participate on equal footing. Integration happens through strand APIs, not exclusive platform deals.

Reputation Services

Aggregate transaction outcomes to provide buyer and seller ratings. Compete on fairness, transparency, and resistance to gaming.

Real-World Impact

This architecture could enable:

  • Small seller empowerment: No platform fees eating into margins
  • Buyer choice: Find products through search that prioritizes your interests, not advertisers'
  • Service innovation: New business models for discovery, delivery, and trust
  • Market efficiency: Better matches between buyers and sellers without platform interference
  • International commerce: Cross-border transactions without platform restrictions

Use Case: Distributed Storage

The Storage Challenge

One of today's common challenges is how users can store all their photos, documents, and other data. The traditional solution is to pay a monthly fee for cloud storage and backups—services like iCloud, Google Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive.

This creates several issues:

  • Recurring costs: Monthly fees that add up over time
  • Vendor lock-in: Moving data between services is cumbersome
  • Privacy concerns: Your data sits on servers you don't control
  • Terms of service risks: Providers can change pricing or terms unilaterally
  • Single point of failure: If the service goes down, your data is unavailable

Sereus: Shared Storage Among Trusted Peers

Sereus is fundamentally about shared databases. Users can form threads for the purpose of backing up and sharing data storage, creating a peer-to-peer storage network among trusted contacts.

The Basic Concept

A user might build or acquire:

  • A cloud server or VPS with substantial storage
  • A physical Linux box with a large hard disk
  • A NAS (Network Attached Storage) device
  • Even a Raspberry Pi with external storage

This device becomes part of the user's operational node cluster (cadre). The cadre automatically shards and distributes data across its nodes.

Collaborative Storage Threads

When two users form a storage thread together, the benefits multiply:

Cross-User Sharding

If two users—each with their own storage infrastructure—form a common storage thread, their data will be sharded across more nodes, spanning both of their cadres. This offers:

  • Greater resilience: Data survives even if entire cadres go offline temporarily
  • Geographic distribution: Data exists in multiple physical locations
  • No single point of failure: Multiple independent infrastructure setups
  • Shared capacity: Participants can leverage each other's storage resources

Role-Based Storage Policies

Thread creators can define storage policies using role-based permissions:

  • Who can store data in the thread
  • How much storage each participant can use
  • What types of data can be stored
  • Retention and archival policies
  • Distribution factors (how many copies to maintain)

Use Cases for Distributed Storage

Personal Backup Networks

Form storage threads with family members or trusted friends. Each participant contributes storage, and everyone benefits from geographic redundancy and increased capacity.

Collaborative Workspaces

Teams can create shared storage threads for project files, with access controlled by roles. As team members come and go, their cadres join or leave the distribution network.

Archive Preservation

Important documents, photos, or historical records can be preserved across multiple participants' storage infrastructure, ensuring long-term availability.

Media Libraries

Families can share photo and video libraries through storage threads, with each member's devices contributing to the total storage pool.

Technical Implementation

Under the hood, Sereus handles the complexity:

  • Optimystic manages block storage and B-trees
  • Fret (the distributed hash table) handles content addressing and chunk location
  • Data is automatically sharded based on the thread's distribution policy
  • Each cadre node stores only the shards assigned to it
  • Queries and retrievals are transparent—applications don't need to know where data is physically stored

Advantages Over Cloud Storage

  • No recurring fees: Pay once for hardware; no monthly subscriptions
  • Privacy by default: Data stored only on devices you and your trusted contacts control
  • Customizable capacity: Add storage as needed by expanding your cadre
  • True ownership: Your data, your infrastructure, your rules
  • Resilient: Data survives individual device failures through distribution
  • Consent-based: Only thread participants have access; no third-party access
  • Geographic distribution: Natural disaster resilience through multi-location storage
  • Flexible policies: Define your own retention, archival, and access rules

Example Scenario

Alice has a home server with 4TB of storage. Bob has a cloud VPS with 2TB. Carol has a desktop computer with 3TB.

  1. They form a storage thread together, agreeing to share backup space
  2. The thread policy allocates each person up to 1.5TB of the collective pool
  3. When Alice stores her family photos, they're automatically sharded across her home server, Bob's VPS, and Carol's desktop
  4. If Alice's home server fails, she can still access all her photos from the shards on Bob's and Carol's systems
  5. Each participant benefits from approximately 9TB of total storage capacity (minus redundancy overhead)

Privacy and Security

  • Data stays within the thread: Only participants' cadres store the data
  • Encrypted distribution: Data is encrypted in transit via libp2p
  • Access control: Role-based permissions determine who can read/write
  • Revocable access: Remove a participant, and their cadre stops receiving updates
  • No third-party access: Unlike cloud providers, there's no company with access to your data
